TONLEE: Theory of Nothingness

An alternative approach to the Theory of Everything.
Here, gravity is not quantizable. Quantum fields emerge in a causally connected regime outside the black hole event horizon—a regime that allows simultaneity at a distance. Inside a black hole, simultaneity is forbidden.

Consequences of the Theory

  • Phenomenal Universe: The universe is not an object but a phenomenon, where natural processes assume the path of causality minimization.
  • Definition of Physical: "Physical" is anything that exists within a causally connected regime as a consequence of allowed simultaneity and entanglement.
  • Information Destruction: Black holes help in the destruction of information. Information, like matter, only emerges in a causally connected spacetime.
  • Black Hole Growth: Black holes do not evaporate; instead, they evaporate the spacetime outside the event horizon, constantly increasing in size even in an isolated universe.
  • Dark Sector Memory: Dark matter represents the intrinsic memory effect of relation (entanglement is a direct instantaneous dark matter memory effect). Dark energy represents the extrinsic memory effect of unrelation.
  • Zero Information: Net information content is 0 in an eternal universe which lacks a unique beginning like a Big Bang singularity.
  • Emergence of Time: Simultaneity allows co-motion (dynamics). When matter forms chunks on a causally connected manifold, quantum mechanics and fields naturally emerge alongside emergent time.
  • Time Dilation: Time appears to move slowly as we move fast because simultaneity becomes difficult to achieve. At the event horizon, time ceases to exist, stripping the information it carried.
